About

I am passionate about mathematics, cryptography and the social value these two subjects generate. Up until now, many roles gave me the opportunity to realize this passion. From private tutor, teaching assistant and (undergraduate/graduate/doctoral) student to university teacher and (post-)doctoral cryptography researcher.

Cryptography brings mathematics to the intersection of information security and privacy. In this regard, cryptography not only protects the integrity of our data, communication, and computation but in a very direct sense also promotes our integrity as individuals and society. I argue that cryptographic technology is a social good.

2024

Shortly after my PhD graduation and subsequent work as post-doctoral cryptography researcher in the Cryptology & Privacy group at IAIK, I ventured to traverse the Andean mountains by bike, from Chile to Colombia.

2023

In 2019, I started my doctoral research at IAIK, Graz University of Technology, in the Cryptology & Privacy group headed by Christian Rechberger. I researched symmetric cryptography, in particular, the design and analysis of arithmetization-friendly cryp­to­graphic primitives and graduated with Highest Honors as PhD in 2023. A main focus of my doctoral research was algebraic crypt­analysis.

In that capacity, I co-designed the following cryptographic primitives:

  • Hades, the cryptographic per­mutation under­lying the Poseidon hash function,
  • the hash-function Reinforced Concrete, and
  • the hash function Monolith.

For a detailed list of my peer-reviewed scientific publications, please see the Digital Bibliography and Library Project, a standard reference and tracker for publications in the field of computer science.

2019

I graduated with Highest Honors as MSc in Mathematics from Paris-Lodron University of Salzburg in 2017. Right after, I joined the IAIK at Graz University of Technology where I mainly worked on the implementation of crypto­graphic algorithms until 2019.
