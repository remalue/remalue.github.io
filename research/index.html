  2. Influence of the Linear Layer on the Algebraic Degree in SP-Networks
    with: Carlos Cid, Aldo Gunsing, Lorenzo Grassi, Christian Rechberger, Markus Schofnegger
    @FSE 2022
    eprint bibtex
    Short abstract Most (keyed or unkeyed) cryptographic permutations admit a representation as a (multivariate) polynomial over a finite field. If the degree of this polynomial representation is too low, the permutation's security is compromised. In this context, we develop tight upper bounds for the degree of permutations based on Sub­sti­tu­tion-Per­mu­ta­tion-Net­works over binary extension fields. Our results allow for well-founded countermeasures against cryptanalysis that leverages the degree of a cryptographic permuation.
  3. An Algebraic Attack on Ciphers with Low-Degree Round Functions: Application to Full MiMC
    with: Maria Eichlseder, Morten Oygarden, Christian Rechberger, Markus Schofnegger, Qingju Wang
    @ASIACRYPT 2020
    eprint DOI bibtex
    Short abstract In recent years, block ciphers and hash functions built from iterated constructions with low-degree round functions have become increasinlgy popular due to their particular efficiency in cryptographic protocols such as Multi-Party Computation, (Fully) Homomorphic Encryption, and Zero-Know­ledge Proofs. We derive a simple upper bound on the degree of iterated Even-Mansour constructions with low-degree round functions and use this upper bound to cryptanalyze the MiMC family of cryptographic permutations. In that capacity, we combine techniques of higher-order differential and interpolation cryptanalysis to recover the secret key from sufficiently many plaintext-ciphertext pairs.
